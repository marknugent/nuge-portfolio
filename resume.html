Experience

June 2020 – Present, San Jose, Calif.

Senior Interaction Designer, Cisco Systems

At Cisco, I worked on two products: Managed Services Accelerator, SaaS focused on managing network services, and since 2022, a network automation product. I collaborated cross-functionally with product managers, product owners, and stakeholders to plan and develop design features.

  • Designed significant portion of the MVP for a SaaS product for cross-domain automation: Competitive review of comparable products; prototype main pages and workflows, collaborated with user testing
  • Prepared user journeys, conceptual diagrams, wireframes, high-fidelity UI mockups, and prototypes
  • Produced annotated design specs and worked closely with engineers throughout development process
Feb. 2018 – May 2020, San Mateo, Calif.

Senior Staff Designer, SiFive

At SiFive, I was UX designer for our SaaS products, primarily Core Designer, web-based software for configuring microprocessor cores, and Chip Designer, a cloud service for assembling components of silicon chips. Core Designer is now used by customers at Qualcomm, Intel, and other well-known companies.

I worked closely with engineers, product managers, executives, and salespeople to design products that solved our users’ problems while supporting our business objectives. My activities at SiFive include user research, UI design, prototyping, visual design, and helping manage our feature roadmap.

  • Served as design lead for SiFive Cloud Services and its Core Designer and Chip Designer SaaS products
  • Design work included wireframes, prototypes, and visual design
  • Assisted engineers by working directly in code (Styled Components, SCSS, HTML, python/Django) to refine the user experience
Nov. 2015 – Apr. 2017, Redwood City, Calif.

Product Designer, Seneca Systems

At Seneca Systems, a company backed by Y Combinator, Initialized Capital, and Cowboy Ventures, I was sole designer for Romulus, a CRM for local governments. Romulus was adopted by city governments nationwide, including Chicago, Houston, Minneapolis, Oakland, Miami, and Boston.

I collaborated with our CEO, CTO, salespersons, and customer-experience staff to set the product's direction. Working closely with engineers in an Agile environment, I managed our project backlog and led planning meetings and sprint retrospectives.

  • Conducted user research; created wireframes, task flows, visual design
  • Worked with developers, customer experience lead, and salespersons
  • After delivering app redesign, paid user count grew fivefold
  • Wrote help documentation and copy for the company’s public website
Nov 2013 – Apr 2015, Arlington, Va.

UX Designer/Developer, i360

At i360, a data analysis company for political strategists, I served as a UX designer, visual designer, and front-end dev in an Agile environment. During my time there, I designed and refined features on a Web-based portal that incorporated various complex functions for gathering and analyzing political data.

I also designed iOS and Android user interfaces for two mobile applications: i360 Walk, which supported door-to-door canvassers, and i360 Calls, which was part of a phone-canvassing system. Both apps were used by thousands of volunteers and garnered praise for from i360’s clients for their ease of use.

  • Conducted user research; created wireframes, task flows, visual design
  • Worked with developers, customer experience lead, and salespersons
  • After delivering app redesign, paid user count grew fivefold
  • Rewrote portal HTML/CSS using Object-Oriented CSS approach
